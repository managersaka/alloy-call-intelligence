# Alloy Membership Policies — as written and practiced

Evidence: 17,751 GHL calls + 1,706 extracted Q&A pairs + the full **103,305-message SMS/email archive** (2024-09 → 2026-07). Where a policy was stated **in writing** by staff, that text is the evidence of record (verbatim quote inventories: `~/alloy-call-intelligence/worker/data/kb-mining/MINED-sms-inbound.md` and `MINED-sms-outbound.md`). Remaining open items → [tbd-gaps](/30-midwest-fitness/reference/knowledgebase/tbd-gaps.md).

**The canonical onboarding statement (SCH new-member text, 2025-12-03) — four core policies in one message:**
> "30-Day Cancellation Notice — based on the cancellation date, you'll be in your final billing cycle or have one more. You have 60 days from the billing date to use sessions before they expire. We can freeze your account for 30 days up to twice a year — your membership will be on hold and pick back up where you left off. 8-Hour Cancellation Notice — give 8 hours notice of a session cancellation or lose the session. We are flexible as long as it's not becoming routine."

## Billing
- Membership is a **recurring 30-day cycle from the activation date** (not a calendar month). **SCH: 2×/wk = 9 sessions/cycle at $449 · 3×/wk = 13 sessions at $539.** A **1×/wk 5-session $259 tier exists as a retention/downgrade option only** ("not a membership we have" for new joins; can't run two cycles in a row as a catch-up downgrade). LINC quotes 4-week/8-or-12-session cycles on calls — per-location convention.
- **Initial contract minimum: 2 months** (SCH in writing 2025-02: "minimum of 2 months, after that month-to-month, cancel anytime with 30-day notice"). LINC 2026 marketing says "no minimum commitment" — reconcile [VERIFY].
- No initiation/signup fee. Payment = card on file; updates taken by phone/text. **HSA/FSA runs through Truemed** ("up to 30% off"; effective ~$30/session SCH). Cash/check unconfirmed [VERIFY].
- Billing errors (double-charges at both studios): refunded/reversed same-day — that's the standard.

## Membership cancellation
- **Exactly 30 days' notice = one final billing cycle, billed at full rate** (19+ written staff statements; zero "28-day" statements in writing — the one verbal "28 days" on a LINC call was wrong; script 30).
- **No cancellation form required**: "Under new Illinois law you only need to provide verbal, text, or email notification to cancel" (in writing 2025-09; a form was used before that).
- The 30-day notice applies **even coming off a freeze**; occasionally **waived via corporate for medical reasons** — but the default is no waiver.
- **Remaining sessions**: usable through the final date (staff even total them: "23 sessions to use between today and 12/26"); forfeited after, with case-by-case ~2-week grace.
- Save attempt is standard: one honest question (freeze? downgrade to $259 1×/wk?) then process without pressure. Downgrades routinely rescue cost-driven cancels.
- Cancel reasons observed (win-back targeting): cost (most common), medical, relocation, competing program, life load.

## Session late-cancel (DISTINCT from membership cancel — members confuse them)
- **Current rule: 8 hours' notice or the session is lost** (in writing since 2025-07; drifted 24h → 12h → 8h across 2024-2025, so long-tenured members remember older numbers).
- **No cash fee ever** — a no-show/late cancel just deducts a session from the cycle.
- No-shows only deducted when there's no prior communication; "flexible as long as it's not becoming routine."

## Session expiration & makeups
- **Sessions expire 60 days from the billing date** (in writing, repeatedly, 2024-12 → 2026-04). Sessions pull **oldest cycle first**; a freeze **pushes expiry back**; "if you're sick or on vacation for a week, you won't lose out."
- Makeup mechanics: add a 3rd session in an existing 2×-week; double up after travel; or a temporary $259 1×/wk downgrade cycle as catch-up.
- **Rules: explain expiration + the 8-hour rule at signup; proactively alert when 3+ unused sessions are <3 weeks from expiry.** Expiration is the #1 dispute source (a 2026-03 20-minute dispute call came from it not being explained at signup).
- If comp sessions/vouchers carry a use-by window (a "7-day voucher window" surprised one member), state it at issuance [VERIFY].

## Pause / freeze (the #1 churn precursor — always escalate to owner/manager)
- **Standard: 30-day freeze, up to twice a year — max 60 total days per rolling year — no charge.** Minimum ~2–3 weeks ("we don't typically freeze for anything less than 21 days"; corporate tightened to ~30-day units Aug 2025).
- Freeze **pushes the billing date AND session-expiration dates back**; membership "picks up where you left off." **No sessions may be used while frozen.** Freeze form filled out in studio.
- **Medical exception: a doctor's/PT note extends the freeze beyond the cap** (a 60→90-day medical freeze was granted) **and preserves a legacy (founders) rate**.
- Never process a pause from a one-line text — 5-minute call first, explore lower frequency (protocol rule).

## Rejoin
- Original/old rate **honored on rejoin** (2025-06 case); founders members grandfathered ($359.20/$431.20 SCH per billing texts). Formal window unstated [VERIFY].
- Zero-guilt tone; fresh InBody on return, movement re-screen optional.

## Referral, guests & discounts
- **The referral program ROTATES by design (Prashant, 2026-07-12) — do not treat any version below as standing; check what's live before quoting.** Current version pending Prashant's confirmation → [tbd-gaps](/30-midwest-fitness/reference/knowledgebase/tbd-gaps.md) #4.

- **Spouse/partner: 10% off** the second membership ($404.10/2x, $485.10/3x SCH quoted 2025-12).
- **Guest policy: up to 2 guests per month; the same guest can't attend more than once** (in writing 2025-03). Guest months ("Share the Love" February, Bring-a-Friend weeks LINC) run periodically.
- **Drop-in rate: $50/session** (raised from $40 in Oct 2025 alongside the founders-rate sunset).

## Escalation rules (unchanged from protocol v2)
Route to owner/manager, never template-only: pause or cancel intent · complaints/billing disputes · injury claims · medical disclosures affecting training · anything you can't answer confidently in one message. "Can we escalate to a manager?" — the answer is always yes, same day.

# Alloy Lead & Member FAQ

## What the channels are for (SMS reality check)
**SMS is the scheduling channel**: ~29% of all inbound texts are session logistics (time-slot coordination alone is 18% — the single biggest bucket). Members confirm with natural language, emoji, and iMessage tapbacks — "Text C to confirm" got 2 bare "C" replies out of 22,382 texts; any automation must parse reactions as confirmations. Phone is where the big lead questions get asked. Response-time expectation set by current practice: **minutes** (SCH median 24.5 min, LINC 5.2 min).

## Top lead questions (Tier 1 — near-every conversation)

**1. "How does it work — is it classes? Personal training?" (315 call asks — the #1 question)**
> "We're small group personal training — not classes with 30 people, not solo 1-on-1 pricing. Never more than 6 people per session, always coach-led, and every exercise is modified to you — everyone gets a movement screen on day one. You pick consistent days and times like a class, but the workout itself is personal training."

**2. "How much does it cost?" (312 call asks + 248 texts)**
Give all three framings unprompted — per session, per month, billing structure — then bridge to the free first session.
> **SCH (current):** "Usually $40–50 per session — 2×/week is $449/mo (9 sessions), 3×/week is $539/mo (13 sessions)."
> **LINC:** "As low as ~$40–42/session depending on frequency — 2×/week $529/mo, 3×/week $629/mo."
Anchor vs 1-on-1: "Elite 1-on-1 training runs $110–120/session around here; we're about a third of that." HSA/FSA (Truemed): "up to 30% more savings."

**3. "What times do you have?" (140 call asks + 40 texts)**
Answer in session-slot terms, not gym-hours terms. SCH: 5a (6a Tu/Th)–noon + 4–7p starts M–F (Fri ends earlier), Sat 7–11a starts. LINC: 6a–noon + 4p–7p last start M–F, Sat mornings last start 11a. Both closed Sunday.

**4. "Where are you located?" (120 call asks + 75 texts)** — landmark script first, then "I'll text you the address." Scripts in the location KBs.

**5. "How long is the first session?" (84 asks)**
> "About an hour — we talk goals, run your InBody scan, do a functional movement screen and a basic workout. It's complimentary."
Regular sessions: **40–60 minutes**. What to wear/bring: comfortable clothes + water bottle (LINC also asks for a change of shoes).

## Tier 2 lead questions

- **Injury / "can I do this with my condition?" (57)** — "You're exactly who we work with. Movement screen + health history day one; coaches modify every exercise around injuries, every session." Medical disclosures → escalate; doctor's clearance requested after surgery/procedures.
- **Ad offers (54)** — know the live offer cold. Current: Free 5-Session VIP Pack (1 assessment + 5 sessions in 30 days, min 2×/wk) at both studios; SCH pilot program (35+/50+). The #1 correction remains: **the SCH 30-Day Challenge was NOT free** ($449 voucher, earned back on results).
- **"Am I signing a contract?" (asked 6+ ways)** — "Month-to-month on a 30-day billing cycle; cancel anytime with 30 days' notice — text or email is enough, no form. Initial minimum is 2 months." (SCH written policy; LINC has marketed "no minimum" — see gaps.)
- **"Can I try it before committing?" (5+)** — complimentary first session, plus whatever multi-session trial is live.
- **"Who will train me? Are trainers certified?" (10+)** — name the actual coach they'd meet; trainers are certified; female trainer available on request.
- **"Do you help with nutrition?" (4+)** — yes: nutrition guidance, EatLove/MyFitnessPal, habit coaching, monthly accountability check-ins are included.
- **Couples/partner (11)** — "Absolutely — same slots work great, and the spouse discount is 10% off the second membership."
- **Older adult / deconditioned (11)** — zero-judgment normalization; SCH runs a dedicated 50+ program campaign.
- **"I'm not leaving my current gym"** — complement positioning (strength here + their cardio there).
