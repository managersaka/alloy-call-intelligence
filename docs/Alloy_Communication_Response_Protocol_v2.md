# Alloy Personal Training — Communication Response Protocol (v2)

**Scope:** Schaumburg & Lincolnshire | **Channels:** Text (SMS), Voice (live + voicemail), Email
**Corpus reviewed:** Feb–Jun 2026 — 9 inbound/outbound lead phone calls (Lincolnshire folder), 20+ SPS transcripts (sampled: Kathryn Oliver, David Ebner, plus catalog scan), accountability transcripts (Irina, Chythannya, plus catalog scan), GHL Conversation Log (June SMS/call classifications, ~20 contacts)
**Known blind spot:** actual SMS message content is still unavailable (GHL messages scope pending re-auth), so member-text taxonomy is inferred from log classifications, not verbatim texts. Phone and consult channels are well-evidenced.

## 0. Response Time Standards

| Group | Text | Voice (missed call) | Email |
|---|---|---|---|
| New leads | **< 5 min** staffed hours; < 1 hr otherwise | Call back < 5 min, then text if no answer | Same business day |
| Past interest (didn't sign) | Same day | Same day | Within 24 hrs |
| Current members | < 2 hrs staffed hours | Same day | Within 24 hrs |
| Previous members | Within 24 hrs | Within 24 hrs | Within 48 hrs |

**Routing alignment (existing GHL classification):** (a) New Lead Sales → Sales owner · (b) Member General Request → Member Care · (c) Accountability → Coach · REVIEW → Manual review same day.

# GROUP 1 — NEW LEADS

## Taxonomy (frequency-weighted from transcripts)

**Tier 1 — appears in nearly every lead conversation:**
1. **Pricing** — asked three ways, each needing a different answer:
   - Per session ("how much per session?")
   - Per month ("so about $400 a month?" — Joyce needed the monthly math done for her)
   - Per week ("so $120 a week?" — Ashwani converted it himself)
2. **"How does it work?"** — model confusion is the norm, not the exception. Observed: "Do you offer group workout classes?" / "So it could be a private class then?" / "You do personal training or how does it work?" Leads arrive assuming either classes (Orangetheory mental model) or 1-on-1 PT. The small-group model must be explained in one breath, every time.
3. **Schedule/hours** — "What times are the classes?" / "Do you have anything at 9 or 10am?" Leads think in class-schedule terms; the answer must convert them to slot terms (6a–11a, 4p–7p+ M–F [VERIFY weekend hours]).

**Tier 2 — common:**
4. **Location** — answered best by landmarks, not addresses: "near the FedEx," "south of Aptakisic on Milwaukee," "by Advanced Dermatology." Each location needs a landmark script. Always follow with "I'll text you the address."
5. **"How long does the first session take?"** — ~1 hour.
6. **Ad-offer questions** — "I saw something on Facebook about a thirty-day thing" (Arlene). Everyone answering phones must know the current ad offers cold.
7. **"How did you hear about us"** intake — Google Maps, Facebook observed; capture name spelling + callback number early ("in case we get disconnected").
8. **Injury/fit concerns** — tennis elbow, neck surgery, stress fracture, chronic low back all observed. Standard answer: movement screen day 1, every exercise modified.
9. **"I'm not leaving my current gym"** — complement positioning (Tricia/Lifetime: strength focus alongside their cardio).

**Tier 3 — recurring but situational:**
10. **Travel/timing deferral** — "I'm in Canada for 20 days, call me after" (Ashwani). Needs a follow-up-date capture habit, not a shrug.
11. **Couples/partner training** — "Would you want to work out with Lori?" (David). Also spouse scheduling for members.
12. **Event-specific training** — half marathon prep (Tricia).
13. **Missed call, no voicemail** — two in the June log alone.
14. **Older-adult/deconditioned profile** — chair rise, stairs, "motivation to get out of a chair" (David). Tone: zero judgment, immediate normalization.

## Templates

**1.1 First touch after form fill (TEXT)**
> Hi {FirstName}, this is {Staff} from Alloy Personal Training {Location}! Thanks for reaching out — excited to hear about your goals. Best first step is a quick 10-min call. Free today at {Time1} or {Time2}?

**1.2 Missed call from lead (TEXT — immediately after callback attempt)**
> Hi, this is {Staff} at Alloy Personal Training {Location} — sorry we missed each other! Happy to answer questions by text or call you back. What works?

**1.3 Pricing (TEXT)**
> Great question — it depends on how often you train. Most people do 2–3x/week, everything is coach-led small group (max 6 people), and sessions run as low as ${X}/session, billed every 4 weeks — so 2x/week is about ${Y}/month [VERIFY current rate card]. No initiation fee, no long-term contract. The first session is complimentary — want to grab a time and see it in person?
>
> *Rule: give all three framings unprompted — per session, per month, and the billing structure. Transcripts show leads doing this math out loud on calls; do it for them.*

**1.4 Pricing (VOICE — live script, matches Colin's observed pattern)**
- "It depends on whether you'd do two or three times a week. We bill every 4 weeks. Sessions can be as low as $[X] — so twice a week is about $[Y] for the month, which works out to $[Z] per session." [VERIFY: quotes observed on calls were "$42–43/session," "2x/week ≈ $423/4wk (~$53/session)"; SPS quoted 3x standard "$629" and founders "$503" — reconcile before scripting]
- Anchor vs 1-on-1: "$100–120/session around here; you get the same customization in a group of 6 max."
- Always bridge: "The first session is complimentary — we sit down, talk goals, run the InBody, do a movement screen. Then you decide."

**1.5 "How does it work / do you have classes?" (VOICE — the model-conversion script)**
> "We're small group personal training — so it's not classes with 30 people, and it's not solo 1-on-1 pricing. Never more than 6 people in a session, always coach-led, and every exercise is modified to you — we do a movement screen with everyone on day one. You pick consistent days and times like a class, but the workout itself is personal training."

**1.6 "How does it work?" (TEXT)**
> We're small group personal training — max 6 people per session, always coach-led. Every workout is customized to you (we do a movement screen day 1), but you get the energy and price point of a group. You pick set days/times that fit your schedule. Want to come see it? First session's complimentary.

**1.7 Schedule/hours (TEXT)**
> We run sessions {hours — e.g., 6–11am and 4–7pm, Mon–Fri} [VERIFY weekend]. You'd pick consistent slots — most people do 2–3x/week — with flexibility when life happens. What times were you thinking?

**1.8 Location (TEXT — per-location landmark scripts)**
> **Lincolnshire:** We're on Milwaukee Ave just south of Aptakisic — near the FedEx, by Advanced Dermatology. Here's the address: {Address + map link}
> **Schaumburg:** {Landmark script — VERIFY} Here's the address: {Address + map link}

**1.9 Ad offer inquiry ("saw your 30-day thing on Facebook") (VOICE — talking points)**
- Confirm the offer plainly; never sound surprised by your own ad.
- Current mechanics: come in for the starting point session first, then the promo period applies [VERIFY current offer terms].
- Convert to booking on the same call: "First step is the assessment — when could you come in this week?"

**1.10 Injury / "is this for me" (TEXT)**
> You're exactly who we work with. Everyone starts with a movement screen and health history, and coaches modify every exercise around injuries — every session. What's going on with your {area}? I'll give you a straight answer on fit.

**1.11 Travel/timing deferral (VOICE + follow-up discipline)**
- "No problem — when are you back? ... Great, I'll reach out on {date}." Log the date in GHL as a task. On {date}, send:
> Hi {FirstName}, {Staff} from Alloy — you mentioned you'd be back around now. Still interested in that complimentary first session? I've got {Time1} or {Time2} this week.

**1.12 Couples/partner (TEXT)**
> Absolutely — training together works great here, and we can put you in the same session slots. Want to book your starting point sessions back-to-back or together?

**1.13 Booking confirmation (TEXT — send immediately after any call that books)**
> Locked in: {Day} {Date} at {Time} at Alloy {Location}, {Address + map link}. Plan on about an hour — we'll talk goals, run your InBody scan, and do a short movement screen. Wear gym clothes. Reply here if anything changes!
>
> *Observed need: leads asked for the address text and a confirmation text unprompted on two of five sampled calls. Make it automatic.*

**1.14 Pre-appointment reminder (TEXT — 24 hrs out; automate)**
> See you tomorrow at {Time}, {FirstName}! Alloy {Location}, {Address}. Reply C to confirm or R to reschedule.

# GROUP 2 — PAST INTEREST THAT DIDN'T SIGN

## Taxonomy

**Tier 1:**
1. **"Which gym is this?"** — stale leads don't remember inquiring (Ashwani, verbatim). Reactivation scripts must re-introduce Alloy in the first sentence and never assume recall.
2. **Retargeting re-entry** — cold leads resurface after seeing a new ad ("I saw you guys again online... something about a thirty day something" — Arlene). Ad campaigns reactivate the dormant list; response scripts must bridge ad → booking in one step.
3. **Price parking** — "once my mindset is there for spending that kind of amount, I'll call you" (Ashwani). Not a no — a deferred yes with a budget hurdle.

**Tier 2:**
4. Post-SPS silence (no stated objection)
5. Schedule objection ("your times didn't work")
6. "Is that intro/founders rate still available?"
7. Timing objections — travel, house purchase, work season
8. "Do I need to redo the consult/InBody?"

## Templates

**2.1 Stale-lead reactivation call (VOICE — opening script, modeled on observed calls)**
> "Hi {FirstName}, it's {Staff} from Alloy Personal Training — we're the small group personal training studio in {Location}. You'd inquired with us a while back and I wanted to see what you were looking for in a gym, and whether it'd make sense to get you in for a complimentary first session."
>
> *Never open with "just following up." Re-establish who you are in sentence one — the transcripts show leads genuinely don't remember.*

**2.2 Stale-lead reactivation (TEXT)**
> Hi {FirstName} — {Staff} from Alloy Personal Training in {Location} (small group personal training, max 6 per session — you inquired with us back in {Month}). We've got {current offer / new schedule openings} and I thought of you. Worth a quick chat?

**2.3 Post-SPS no-response, day 2 (TEXT)**
> Hi {FirstName}, {Staff} from Alloy. Really enjoyed our session — your starting point was genuinely solid. I'm holding a spot in the {Day/Time} group we discussed. Want me to lock it in, or is there a question I can answer first?

**2.4 Post-SPS no-response, day 7 (TEXT)**
> Hi {FirstName} — no pressure, just closing the loop. If timing isn't right, totally fine; if something specific is holding you back (schedule, price, anything), tell me straight and I'll see what I can do. Your InBody results stay on file either way.

**2.5 Price-parked lead (follow-up cadence)**
- On the call: "Totally understand. Can I check back in {30/60} days?" → log the date.
- At the date (TEXT):
> Hi {FirstName}, {Staff} from Alloy — you asked me to circle back around now. No pitch, just the facts: {current best offer / promo}, first session still complimentary. Want a time?
- If a promo launches before the date, the promo message replaces the check-in — retargeting demonstrably reactivates this list.

**2.6 Price objection (VOICE — talking points)**
- Acknowledge: "Fair — it's a real commitment."
- Reframe frequency: 2x/week costs meaningfully less than 3x; consistency beats volume.
- Anchor to their current spend (leads from Lifetime were paying $120/session 1-on-1).
- If a promo exists, state its deadline once, honestly. No manufactured urgency.

**2.7 Schedule-objection resurface (TEXT — when new slots open)**
> Hi {FirstName} — you mentioned {early mornings/evenings} were the blocker. We just opened a {Time} {Day} group at {Location}. Thought of you first — want the spot?

**2.8 Re-engagement 30–60 days (EMAIL)**
> Subject: Your InBody results are still on file, {FirstName}
>
> Hi {FirstName},
>
> {Staff} from Alloy {Location} — you came in back in {Month} and we talked about {goal}. Genuine check-in: how's it going?
>
> If you found something that works, honestly, great. If you're where you were (or motivation faded — the most common thing we hear), your baseline scan is on file and your first session back is on us. We'd re-scan and go from there.
>
> Reply here or text {Number}.
>
> {Staff}

# GROUP 3 — CURRENT MEMBERS

## Taxonomy (June log + accountability transcripts)

**Tier 1 — daily volume:**
1. Session scheduling/rescheduling (most common single category in the log)
2. Absence notifications — illness, travel (Amanda; Chythannya's back flare-up)
3. Appointment confirmations, incl. emoji-only replies (log as confirmed; no reply needed)

**Tier 2 — weekly volume:**
4. Membership pause/hold requests (Jeanne) — **churn precursor, always escalate**
5. InBody scan scheduling + accuracy questions ("how accurate is that thing?" — Irina, verbatim)
6. Nutrition/habit questions — protein targets, water, MyFitnessPal setup, calorie goals (Irina, Chythannya)
7. App access — workout/mobility program assignments not showing (Irina)
8. **Session package mechanics** — "you've got six sessions that expire May 11, five that expire June 11" (Chythannya). Members will ask: how many do I have, when do they expire, can I catch up? Needs a fast-lookup answer and a proactive expiring-sessions alert.
9. Catch-up scheduling after missed weeks ("3x/week for the next month and a half to get caught up")

**Tier 3:**
10. Billing questions (charge dates, card updates)
11. 24-hr cancellation policy / late cancels
12. Event/seminar questions + RSVP (habit seminar w/ QR code; June broadcast reminders)
13. Guest/referral requests
14. Spouse/partner joint scheduling
15. Progress questions between check-ins ("am I on track?")

## Templates

**3.1 Reschedule (TEXT)**
> No problem, {FirstName}! Your {Day/Time} is released. I've got {Option1} or {Option2} this week — which works? (You're within the 24-hr window, so all good.)

**3.2 Illness/injury absence (TEXT)**
> Feel better, {FirstName}! You're marked out — just rest. When you're back, text me and we'll build a catch-up plan so those sessions don't expire on you. Ease-back note for the coaches is already in your file.

**3.3 Late cancel/no-show, first occurrence (TEXT)**
> Missed you at {Time} today, {FirstName} — hope everything's okay! This one's on the house since it's not like you, but heads-up: we need 24 hrs' notice to release the spot. Want a makeup this week?

**3.4 Session balance / expiration question (TEXT)**
> Quick look: you've got {N} sessions that expire {Date} and {N} more starting {Date}. To use them all, let's do {frequency} through {Date} — want me to book the slots now?
>
> *Proactive rule: when a member's unused sessions are 3+ with under 3 weeks to expiry, send this unprompted. Expired sessions are the quietest form of churn.*

**3.5 Membership pause request (TEXT — first response; then escalate)**
> Absolutely, we can work with you, {FirstName}. Before I process anything — is this a "life is crazy for a few weeks" pause or a longer break? Sometimes dropping to {lower frequency} keeps the habit alive at lower cost and keeps your rate lock. 5-min call so I get it right? [VERIFY pause policy terms]
>
> *Rule: never process a pause from a one-line text. Route to owner/manager. Pause requests are the #1 churn precursor.*

**3.6 Pause confirmation (EMAIL)**
> Subject: Your Alloy membership pause — confirmed
>
> Hi {FirstName}, confirming: paused {StartDate}–{EndDate}. Billing stops {Date}, resumes {Date}, your {rate} rate stays locked, and your {Day/Time} slots are held until {Date} [VERIFY slot-hold]. While you're out, keep the mobility work going in the Alloy app (15–20 min, no equipment) — it makes the return week far easier. You're on the calendar for {ReturnDate}.

**3.7 InBody scheduling (TEXT)**
> You're due! Scans take 5 min — easiest 10 min before your next session ({Day/Time} → scan at {Time-0:10}?). For a consistent read: no food ~2 hrs before, normal hydration, same time of day as last scan.

**3.8 InBody accuracy question (TEXT — verbatim-tested answer from the floor)**
> Fair question — no scan is perfect, but the InBody is as good as it gets outside a DEXA, and here's the part that matters: we always use the same machine, same conditions. So even if any single reading has noise, the *trend* is real. That's why we compare every 4–6 weeks instead of obsessing over one number.

**3.9 Nutrition/habit question (TEXT)**
> Love that you're asking. Short version: {one-liner — protein ≈ 1g per lb of fat-free mass; water ≈ half body weight in oz}. Track just one typical day in MyFitnessPal before your next check-in and we'll work from real numbers instead of guessing — want me to send the setup link?

**3.10 App/workout access (TEXT)**
> On it. {If assigned:} It's under Workouts → {Program} — force-close and reopen the app. {If not:} Just assigned it now. Screenshot me if it's still not showing and I'll fix it today.

**3.11 Billing (TEXT)**
> Your membership bills every 4 weeks — current cycle {Date}–{Date}, next charge {Amount} on {Date}. Anything look off, tell me and I'll dig in today.

**3.12 Event/seminar RSVP (TEXT — broadcast + reply handling)**
> Broadcast: {Event} at Alloy {Location}, {Date/Time} — {one-line value}. Free for members; save your spot: {link/QR}.
> Reply handling: any affirmative reply → "You're in! {Date/Time}, see you there." Log RSVP in GHL.

**3.13 Guest/referral (TEXT)**
> We'd love that! {GuestName}'s first session is free — movement screen, InBody, full workout. Best fit is your {Day/Time} group so you're together. And when a referral joins, you get {reward} [VERIFY].

# Voice Standards (all groups)

- **Live answer:** 3 rings staffed hours. "Thank you for calling Alloy Personal Training in {Location}, this is {Name}, how may I assist you?" (This is the exact observed greeting — keep it; it works.)
- **Intake discipline on every lead call** (observed pattern to standardize): name + spelling → callback number "in case we get disconnected" → "how did you hear about us?" → goals → injuries → schedule window → book the starting point session → text address + confirmation.
- **Missed call, any group:** callback within SLA + immediate text. A text within 5 minutes salvages most missed calls; silence loses the lead.
- **Every call that books gets two automatic texts:** address/confirmation immediately, reminder 24 hrs out.
- **Voicemail greeting:** "You've reached Alloy Personal Training {Location}. We're on the training floor with members — leave your name and number and we'll call back within the hour, or text this number for a faster reply."

# Escalation Rules

Route to owner/manager (never template-only):
- Pause or cancellation intent
- Complaints (coach, billing dispute), injury claims
- Medical disclosures affecting training (new diagnosis, surgery, pregnancy)
- Anything the responder can't answer confidently in one message
